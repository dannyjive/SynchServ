White Center Food Bank Relocation

Client: White Center Food Bank

The White Center Food Bank Relocation project represents a landmark achievement in community resilience and financial stewardship, transitioning the organization from years of displacement into a permanent, debt-free home in the heart of White Center. Completed in late 2025 following a capital campaign that exceeded its $8.6M goal by $1.5M, the new facility provides a dignified and expanded environment specifically designed to meet surging local demand. The project prioritized mission-critical functional upgrades, including optimized client intake systems and an expanded pantry layout curated for culturally familiar foods. By securing a high-capacity asset without the burden of debt, this relocation ensures operational longevity and serves as a generational engine for food justice, proving that strategic capital planning is the foundation for sustainable community belonging.

Hillside Paradise Parking Plots & Commercial Teaching Kitchen

Client: World Relief Western Washington

The Hillside Paradise Parking Plots project exemplifies the reclamation of underutilized infrastructure into a vital community asset by transforming a dormant asphalt parking lot into a productive 50-plot garden and a high-impact teaching kitchen. This dual-phase capital improvement addresses critical food security for local refugee and immigrant populations through a sophisticated depaving effort and sustainable Hügelkultur systems, while the interior commercial kitchen serves as the essential processing hub that closes the loop between urban agriculture and economic empowerment. Engineered for vocational training and entrepreneurial incubation, the kitchen renovation required a seamless integration of technical infrastructure—including high-capacity ventilation and health-code-compliant finishes—into the existing building footprint.

Saint Mark's Episcopal Cathedral Exterior Facade Upgrades & Window Replacement

Client: Saint Mark's Episcopal Church

The Saint Mark's Episcopal Cathedral Exterior Facade Upgrades and Window Replacement project is a landmark example of architectural stewardship, balancing historic preservation with high-performance technical upgrades. The scope centered on a comprehensive building envelope restoration, replacing the nave windows with energy-efficient, color-matched glazing and cladding the previously bare concrete walls in Indiana limestone to seamlessly integrate with the cathedral’s existing 1997 masonry. This complex transformation extended to critical accessibility and site improvements, including the addition of a new elevator, an enhanced entry terrace, and the installation of a symbolic Celtic cross. Managing the technical weight of a fully operational site, the project required rigorous safety protocols and specialized oversight for a unique scaffolding system, all while maintaining constant coordination with the adjacent art school and neighborhood stakeholders. The result is a revitalized, weather-resilient facility that honors the cathedral's aesthetic legacy while expanding its capacity for inclusive community engagement.
